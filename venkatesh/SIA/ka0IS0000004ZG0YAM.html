1. Celebratory Cake Requests – General Policy

a. The celebratory cake minimum block time* requirement is strictly 3h 30mins. This information can be made known to the public.

b. For requests made by FCL/JCL passengers, agents are empowered to accede, for flights with a minimum block time* of 1hr 27mins. This information should not be made known to the public.
 
block time* - Reflected as flight duration in ARD
 
c. Personalization of cake wording is not available for all celebratory cakes.
 
d. Caterers will cater cakes according to their capability. SQ does not customize cakes in accordance with passenger’s preference. This includes the customization of flavours and ingredients (vegetarian, gluten free, etc.)

 

e.  Staff traveling either on duty or privilege ticket or sub-load ticket are not allowed to request for a cake.

 


2. Birthday Cake Policy


a.  The BCAKE policy is strictly +/- 1 day from birthday. This information can be made known to the public.
 ​​​​​
 
b.   For requests made by FCL/JCL passengers or PPS members (regardless of class of travel),
  • agents are empowered to accede, if flight is within +/-3 days from passenger’s birthday. This information can be made known to the public.
 
c.  For requests made by HV passengers**, 
  • agents are empowered to accede, if flight is within +/- 7 days from passenger’s birthday, regardless of class of travel. This information should not be made known to the public.
 
​​​​​d. Requests made beyond the stipulated allowance period are to be strictly rejected, regardless of class of travel or KF tier.

** For HV passenger, please accede to request and route it to IS&D to arrange for uplift.

 
​​​​e. BCAKE requests made at least 24 hours before flight departure can be acceded to by contact centre agents as long as the meal response is reflected as HK.



 
3. Anniversary/ Honeymoon Cake Policy
a. Anniversary cakes are intended for relationship anniversary celebrations (e.g wedding, engagement, etc). Staff do not need to request for documentation to verify the request.

b. Staff should request one anniversary or honeymoon cake for a couple.

c. Requests made for more than one sector are not allowed. Staff must check passenger’s preference and indicate the preferred sector.
 
E.g., Anniversary cake for passenger travelling from Singapore to San Francisco.
Passenger should select whether the cake is to be served on either SIN-HKG or HKG-SFO, not both.
Note: If no sector is being selected, passenger will receive two cakes for their flight which is incorrect.
 
 

   d. ACAKE/HCAKE requests made at least 24 hours before flight departure can be acceded to by contact centre agents as long as the meal response is reflected as HK.
