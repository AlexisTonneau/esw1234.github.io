1.Economy Lite fare V/K and Economy Value Fare Q/N Reservation Booking Designator (RBD)
 

a. Lite V K Fare condition states that there is no date change/ flight changes/ re-route, or cancellation allowed. Passengers will need to purchase a new ticket to get on a different flight should they decide to make voluntary changes.

 

b. Ticket change condition for VK is restrictive and no-show is not applicable (i.e., if passenger does not show up for the flight, ticket is considered used). 

 

c. The VK fares are non-refundable. 

 

d. While ticket changes are not allowed for VK, the value fare type in QN offers more flexibility in the change and cancellation conditions.

 

e. Current 25% tier bonus on actual miles flown will be maintained for all tiers (PPS, EG, ES). This tier bonus will not apply to Economy Value/ Lite/ Group (QNVKG RBD) tickets.

3. FAQ

(I)          General 

Q. Why has Singapore Airlines opted to separate Lite fares and introduce a new fare type?
A. Our product offerings have changed over time to address changing needs of different customer segments appropriately. The new Economy Value fare type we have introduced offers more flexibility in the change and cancellation conditions.

 

Q. Will the new fare type / fare condition changes be rolled out across all points of sale or will they be implemented gradually in stages?
A. The new fare type and fare condition changes will be rolled out across all points of sale simultaneously.

 

Q. How can we differentiate whether the old or new fare conditions are applied to any particular ticket?
A. The ticket issuance/reissuance date will determine the conditions which apply to the ticket. For tickets issued before the change, the first change/reissuance will follow fees of the old fare conditions. Subsequent changes will be according to new fare conditions.

AROs / TAs can check the applicable ticket conditions via ARD/GDS. Passengers can check the applicable conditions via the “Manage Booking” flow.

 

Q. With the introduction of the new fare type, will passengers be able to self-service online in the event of schedule change or disruption (e.g. UN / TK)?
A. Yes, passengers will still be able to perform self-service online for such situations.

 

Q. Will there be any changes to the RBD used for exceptional handling in “Why Not” circumstances (e.g. medical / compassionate cases where we now book on W-cls)?
A. No, there are no changes to the RBD usage for exceptional handling.

 

Q. Will the fares be auto-priced through the system?
A. Yes, the fares will be filed with the respective distribution channels and be auto-priced through the system. 

 

Q. If there is a system glitch and the ticket is unable to be issued in time with the old set of fare conditions, is there an avenue for appeal?
A. The usual escalation process will apply, please connect with your SIA Sales Ops contact. 

 

 

(II)          Change / Cancellation / No Show / Go Show

 Q. Why are ticket changes not allowed for VK?
A. The fare types have been recalibrated to address different customer needs more effectively. While ticket changes are not allowed for VK, we have also introduced a new fare type in QN which offers more flexibility in the change and cancellation conditions.

 

Q. If I buy a combination of fare types (e.g., outbound on V-cls fare where ticket change is not allowed and inbound on E-class fare where change is free), can I change my entire itinerary?
A. For changes to the entire itinerary, the stricter rules/conditions apply. In the case above, V-cls does not allow change, hence, outbound sector cannot be changed. However, you will still be able to change the inbound sector on E-class for free.

[Internal] Change condition applies by inbound or outbound sector based on the sector which is being changed. If the entire itinerary is being changed, the stricter rules/conditions apply. Conditions for cancellation will always follow that of the more restrictive fare type.

In the summary of fare conditions for SAA booking flow, a clause on the rules governing different fare types combined for inbound/outbound will be shown.

 

Q. If I buy a combination of fares (e.g. outbound on Economy Lite and return on Economy Flexi fares), is it possible to fully refund or partially refund my flights?
A. Ticket refund conditions are based on the stricter set of fare rules/conditions in the itinerary. As Economy Lite does not allow for refund, you may not refund your itinerary. If you require a fare which allows refundability, we have other fare types which offer different degrees of flexibility for refund (e.g., purchasing Economy Value + Economy Flexi will allow refund of partial/entire itinerary for a fee).

 

Q. If I purchase an Economy Lite fare and there is a subsequent need to change the flight, do I have to purchase a new ticket?
A. Ticket changes/refunds are not allowed for Economy Lite fares. You will have to purchase a new ticket for your trip. If you require a fare which allows flexibility for change of flight, we have other fare types which offer different degrees of ticket change flexibility.

 

Q. If I buy VK fare where ticket change is not allowed and I need to change my flight, do I have to purchase a new ticket and let the original purchase go to waste?
A. Ticket change is not allowed for VK fare. If you require a fare which allows flexibility for change of flight, we have other fare types which offer different degrees of ticket change flexibility.

 

Q. If I buy VK fare where ticket change is not allowed and I missed my flight, can I use the ticket to rebook to a next flight?
A. If you missed your flight, you will not be able to use the ticket to rebook to another flight as ticket change is not allowed for VK fare. You will need to purchase a new ticket for the new flight.

 

Q. If I buy VK fare where ticket change is not allowed, can I pay fare difference and/or penalty fee to change/upgrade my ticket to a fare in a higher RBD and/or cabin on the same flight?
A. VK fare is not eligible for ticket change to higher RBD and/or cabin. You will need to purchase a new ticket for the higher RBD and/or cabin.

 
 

(III)          Free Baggage Allowance

Q. Why is SQ reducing the free baggage allowance for YBE and QNVK?
A. The fare types have been recalibrated to address different customer needs more effectively. While the free baggage allowance has been streamlined across the fare types, we have also introduced a new fare type in QN which offers more flexibility in the change and cancellation conditions. The change in free baggage allowance change applies only to weight concept, there is no change to the free baggage allowance for piece concept.

 
 

(IV)          Corporate Traveler

Q. How will this change impact my travel / airline program?
A. Singapore Airlines is pleased to inform corporates which are on the Singapore Airlines Corporate Program will not be affected by these changes. The existing corporate rates and discounts that are currently being offered will continue to be extended to the corporates on the Standard and Flexi fare types.

 

Q For corporate travelers, are there any exceptions to the new fare rules and ticket conditions for Economy Lite fare type?
A. There will not be exceptions to the new fare rules and ticket conditions of Economy Lite fare type.

 

Q. Will there be discounts applied to the Economy Value fare classes in my global corporate airline program with SIA?
A. There will be no discounts accorded for Economy Value fare classes in the global corporate agreement.
