PORTABLE ELECTRICAL / ELECTRONIC EQUIPMENT

The use of any portable electrical/electronic device may interfere with the operation of the aircraft’s navigation and communication equipment. To minimise the risks, such devices if determined to be non-intentional transmitters or their transmissions inhibited, can be switched on when the aircraft is in cruise or when the aircraft door is open. These devices must not be used during taxi, take-off and landing.

Electrical medical devices that have been determined not to interfere with aircraft navigation and communication equipment can be used during all phases of flight.  Regulations allow two kinds of electrical medical devices to be operated at all times on board:

a)    Portable Hearing Aids

b)    Heart Pacemakers

 

Other than the two above, the carriage of medical devices has to be approved by Technical Services, Engineering.

Note: For flights to and from US, the US DOT Part 382 rules apply. Please refer to US PRM Ruling under PRM “SOP”.

 


Approval to Carry Medical Portable Electrical/Electronic Device (MPED) on Board 

A copy of the MPED Checklist should be completed by booking station after consulting customer and emailed together with the equipment specifications to:

(i)            Tech_Svcs@singaporeair.com.sg – Attn: Manager Technical Services (Avionics)

(ii)           FS_Reports@singaporeair.com.sg

 

In addition to include anyone of the following key words in the Email Subject when submitting M-PED request:

    • MPED
    • M-PED
    • Medical Portable Electronic Device

 

This is to facilitate the routing to the relevant stakeholders in Engineering.

Upon approval, Technical Services will forward the checklist to Safety, Security & Environment, Flight Safety Department for further evaluation. Flight Safety’s approval is not required if the subject medical device is not approved by Technical Services, Engineering.

Please allow a lead-time of 14 working days for the approval process. 

 
 

Conditions for Carriage without Technical Services’ approval 

The following types of MPEDs do not require Technical Services’ approval

a)    Nebulizers

b)    Sleep Apnea Machine/Continuous Positive Airway Pressure Device (CPAP)

c)     FAA-approved Portable Oxygen Concentrator (POC)

 

While approval is not needed, staff should advise the passenger that as much as possible, the MPED should be self-powered, as the Inseat Power Supply (ISPS) may not be available on the passenger’s flight(s). The battery should be leak-proof and must either be integrated inside the equipment or connected to a separate battery pack by a short cable with a properly insulated electrical plug. Batteries with exposed terminals and 'crocodile clip' type connections are not allowed.

For more details, please refer to the relevant section below for each of these devices. 

 
 

Carriage of Nebulizers on board
SIA no longer requires passengers to send their battery-powered nebulizers to Singapore for pre-flight testing before bringing them on board. Technical Services’ approval for use of nebulizers is also no longer needed. If a passenger informs us of his/her intention to use a nebulizer during the flight, staff should:

a)    Advise that for safety reasons, use of the nebulizer is restricted to be used during flight cruise only. Use of the nebulizer during take-off, landing or when the seat belt sign is “ON”, could interfere with aircraft navigation equipment.

 

b)    Advise the passenger to bring along his/her own asthma inhaler (non-battery operated) for use during take-off/landing if required.

 

c)     Inform the passenger that subject to availability and compatibility, the aircraft’s In seat Power Supply (ISPS) may be used. Staff can check on the availability and compatibility under the ‘Seat and Cabin Product Information’. Even if ISPS is compatible, the availability of power is not guaranteed throughout the whole flight as power could be interrupted or turned off during the flight. It is best to advise the passenger that the CPAP should be self-powered using gel or dry battery (e.g., lithium or lithium ion cells or batteries)

Ensure that the passenger is not assigned to an Emergency Exit Row seat.

 
 

Carriage of Sleep Apnea Machine (CPAP) on board

The usage of the CPAP (Continuous Positive Airway Pressure) Sleep Apnea Machine is now allowed onboard SIA flights.  Technical Services’ approval for use of the CPAP during the flight (including using the Inseat Power Supply (ISPS) to power the device) is no longer needed.

Note: For flights to and from US, the US DOT Part 382 rules apply. Please refer to US PRM Ruling under PRM “SOP”.

If a passenger informs us of his/her intention to use a CPAP during the flight, staff should:

a)    Check if the CPAP is used for sleep disorder or for treatment of other medical conditions. If CPAP is for treatment of any other medical condition, normal MEDA procedures are to be followed.

b)    Advise that for safety reasons, the use of the CPAP is restricted to be used during cruise only. Use of the CPAP during take-off, landing or when the seat belt sign is “ON”, could interfere with aircraft’s navigation equipment.

c)     Inform the passenger that subject to availability and compatibility, the aircraft’s Inseat Power Supply (ISPS) may be used. Staff can check on the availability and compatibility under the ‘Seat and Cabin Product Information’ section of ERIC. Even if ISPS is compatible, the availability of power is not guaranteed throughout the whole flight as power could be interrupted or turned off during the flight. It is best to advise the passenger that the CPAP should be self-powered using gel or dry battery (e.g., lithium or lithium ion cells or batteries).
