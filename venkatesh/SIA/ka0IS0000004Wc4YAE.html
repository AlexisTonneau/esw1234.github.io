TLC (Tender Loving Care) represents a conscious effort by SIA to actively identify opportunities to offer an elevated and memorable experience to specific groups of passengers (including their travel companions) who may have unique needs.

Who are TLC passengers?
Passengers who self-declare that they are:
 
  • Pregnant women
  • Elderly passengers
  • Family with infants / young children
  • Passengers with impaired abilities / mobility, hidden disabilities

DO NOT explicitly ask passenger if they fit the description of TLC passenger.

Broadly, under this SOP, staff should attentively listen out for cues when passengers communicate their requests for assistance and accord flexibility with seating / offer MAAS services that are relevant to their needs.

 
AreaWhat staff can do
Seats
  • Accord flexibility to offer appropriate seats with fee waiver (excluding EMEX) that will better meet passenger’s needs. Refer here
MAAS
  • Offer MAAS that is relevant to passenger’s needs. Refer here


Listed below are a few examples of passengers who can be accorded TLC:
  • Assist with Seat Request:
    • Passenger who had a knee operation and requests for an aisle seat
    • Passenger requesting for a forward zone seat due to motion sickness
    • KFEG member who was soft-landed to KFES recently and request for Advance seat selection in the forward zone
    • Caller advised that her mother has Crohn's disease and needs to go to the lavatory frequently
    • Passenger is travelling on compassionate grounds and is overcome with anxiety
  • Request for MAAS:
    • Passenger is dyslexic and may need help to get to the departure gate
    • Elderly passenger who may be disoriented at times and may get confused to get to the aircraft
