Q. Why did we create the new meal concept?
A. SIA has engaged Fjord to do a study focusing on short-haul flights with the following objectives:
  • Improve the inflight meal experience
  • Map processes and find areas to improve
An ideation workshop was held to address customer feedback regarding meals on short-haul flights:
  • Lack of variety in main courses
  • Dry food due to overheating
The selected idea also had to be:
  • Sustainable and eco-friendly
  • Efficient for cabin crew
  • Cost efficient

Q. What is unique about the new meal concept?
A. Allow us to offer a wider variety of main courses in YCL, especially soupy comfort food.
  • Oven able and leak-proof box allows us to offer soupy and gravy dishes (e.g. congee, chunky soup, noodles with gravy, etc.)
Improve quality of food.
  • Shape and design of box retains heat better
  • Material of box also makes main course more resistant to overheating, hence preserving quality of food
An eco-friendly and hygienic solution.
  • Paper is from sustainable sources (main course box and cup have FSC certification)
  • Cutlery is made from bamboo
  • All items on tray, including food waste, can be converted to Refuse-Derived Fuel (RDF) through eco-digester procured by SATS. RDF can be used as an alternative source of energy to fossil fuel and coal.
  • Minimise SUPs on the tray
  • Safe packaging enhances food safety and hygiene


Q. Is this new meal concept called “Project Bento”?
A. “Project Bento” is only an internal project title and not to be shared with pax. To pax, it is called “New Economy Class Meal Concept for Short-Haul Flights”.

Q: Which flights are these going to be available on?
A. The new meal concept will be available on flights < 3.5h on SQ, excluding KUL, PEN, KNO.

Q: Why is the new meal concept not available on flights to KUL, PEN, and KNO?
A. Due to the short flight time, a full meal service is not available on flights between Singapore and Kuala Lumpur, Penang and Medan.

Q: Why is the new meal concept not available on flights to the Philippines?
A. In our Flight recategorization project, MNL is classified under CAT3, i.e. more than 3.5hrs. Hence, it is ok if MNL does not follow the rest of the SEA flights in terms of meal design. In addition, we considered that MNL has major traffic jams and many, if going to other parts of Philippines, will need to travel on the road after landing. A large casserole is more substantial for the passengers as it will hold more food, especially in view of the removal of appetisers for all Economy Class meal services.

Q: Will there be plans to launch the new meal concept for flights longer than 3.5 hours?
A. Currently, there are no plans to launch this for flights longer than 3.5 hours.

Q: Why is the new meal concept available only on flights double catered from Singapore? Are there any plans to cater this ex-station?
A. The new meal concept is available on double-catered flights to allow the waste to be sent back to Singapore and into the eco-digester at SATS. For now, this includes stations that were catering pre-Covid e.g. BKK and SGN. ISD will inform station on the arrangement for the new normal in due course.

Q: What exactly is the new about the meal concept?
A. The unique oven-able, leak-proof box holds the same amount of food as the casserole but is deeper and more secure, allowing it to retain gravy and soupy dishes without any seepage. This allows us to introduce a greater variety of main courses, including Singaporean favourites such as congee, laksa, and gravy-rich mee siam, that were not previously available in Economy Class. We have also introduced new desserts that feature popular local flavours such as Ondeh Ondeh Cake and Pulut Hitam Cake. In total, more than 40 new dishes (including desserts) were created for the new meal concept.

Q: What has been done to create awareness for the new meal concept?
A. We have created a stop motion video for social media. This is posted on Singapore Airlines’ Facebook page. Passengers may also find out more about the new meal concept via SAA. Inflight, a liner on the digital menu will inform Economy Class pas that the flight features the new meal concept from 1 Dec 2020.
