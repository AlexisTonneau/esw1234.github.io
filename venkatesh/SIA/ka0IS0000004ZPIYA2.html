Background

If passenger's baggage exceeds the free baggage allowance (FBA), additional baggage charges will be applied.

  • Refer to SAA for the FBA for
    • weight concept - Non-Canadian/US routes in the PNR
    • piece concept- Canadian/US routes in the PNR
Non-US/Canada routesUS/Canada-routes
Passenger can pre-purchase additional baggage in blocks of 5kg up to a maximum of 100kg per flight.

Passenger can pre-purchase any combination of the following

  • Up to 2 additional baggage pieces in addition to the number of pieces allowed for free

Or

  • additional weight for each piece of baggage within the free baggage allowance that exceeds the weight limit in the class of travel

Or

  • oversize baggage for each piece of baggage within the free baggage allowance that its sum of length, weight and height exceeds 158cm

Scenarios where pre-purchase of additional baggage is not allowed 

  1. PNR with another airline’s operating or codeshare segment(s) as part of the itinerary
  2. PNR ticketed on another airline’s ticket
  3. PNR for group bookings
  4. PNR with any waitlisted segment
  5. PNR that is partially ticketed, including partially ticketed passenger(s) or segment(s)
  6. For individual segments when there is no stopover for connecting flight segments
  7. For carry-on baggage
  8. For baggage on extra-paid seat (CBBG)
  9. Individual passenger with 2 adjacent seats, i.e., extra seat service (EXST), for personal comfort
  10. Pet carriage


For above scenarios, inform passenger that additional baggage charges will be levied upon airport check-in. Refer to SAA under Additional Baggage Charges.

Rates on ARD
Staff are reminded NOT to use the ‘Excess Baggage Rates’ link in ARD’s home page to check excess baggage rates for SQ flights.

  • The “Excess Baggage Rates’ link should only be used only for OAL flights.


Additional baggage rates for SQ flights are only available from the catalogue which is triggered from the ‘Add Service’ link from a priced or ticketed PNR.

  • The amount reflected will include passenger’s KF tier (if KF member details are added into the PNR) and/or flight discount (if applicable).
  • Itinerary needs to be priced or ticketed to use ‘Price services’ for the correct amount.


For general enquiries on generic pre-purchase discounted pricing, staff should encourage passengers to self-service and check on SAA.

 

Additional Purchase of XBAG
Passenger have pre-purchased additional baggage (XBAG). However, he/she may want to add on more later to accommodate more weight/luggage.

To illustrate, passenger purchased 20kg XBAG. He/ She now request to top up 5kg to make the XBAG to 25kg.
For each segment, only 1 SSR XBAG element is allowed. Refer below table for the handling.

For flights more than 48hrs to departure

 

For flights within 48 – 6hrs to departure

a. Inform passenger that top up of XBAG is not possible. Passenger to pay for the total XBAG required and the current XBAG will be refunded. Refer to this article for reference on the turnaround time.

 

b. Delete the current XBAG EMD on the PNR.

 

c. Issue the new XBAG.

 

d. Escalate to L2 to process the refund of the original EMD.

 

Inform passenger that top up of XBAG is not possible.

a. Refund the original XBAG that passenger purchased with higher discounted rates.

 

Purchase total XBAG that he/she requires, as discounted under Last Minute Purchase.

 

 

b. Passenger can pay for additional weight/piece upon airport check-in. There will be no discounted rates.

 

 


Waiver 
There is a no waiver policy for additional baggage.

  • If there is a valid reason to escalate passenger's request, staff to request for supporting document(s).
     

Upon receipt of the document (if any), check the document information tallies with the reason for the reques

  • If everything is in order, escalate to
    • MCCO/MCC/MSC for Contact Centres and ION Service Centre
    • GM/Area/State/Station Manager for respective stations.


Upon approval, inform passenger his actual baggage entitlement, and that a one-off waiver has been granted as goodwill. This baggage waiver is to be recorded in ARD or CM. Staff to include the authoriser’s title, not name.

ARD
  • The remark should be segment-related.
CM


a. Under the “Free Text Comment”, staff must not enter any personal details or information which could relate back to authoriser, staff or passengers, such as names, KrisFlyer numbers, personal identification numbers, etc. 


b. Under the “Reasons for Waiver”, staff to use the available entries in the dropdown list.
When “Others” is selected, to add clear and generic reasons for baggage waivers under the “Free Text Comment”.
